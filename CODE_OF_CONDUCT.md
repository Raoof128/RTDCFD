# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our
community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body
size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender
identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status,
nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity
and orientation.

## Our Standards

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inclusive environment for all
participants. We expect all community members to follow these standards:

### Expected Behavior

- **Be Respectful**: Treat others with respect, professionalism, and kindness
- **Be Inclusive**: Welcome and include all contributors regardless of background
- **Be Collaborative**: Work together constructively and share knowledge
- **Be Professional**: Maintain professional conduct in all interactions
- **Be Helpful**: Provide assistance and support to fellow community members
- **Be Ethical**: Uphold ethical standards in cybersecurity research and practice

### Unacceptable Behavior

- **Harassment**: Any form of harassment, including sexual harassment
- **Discrimination**: Discrimination based on any protected characteristic
- **Intimidation**: Threatening or intimidating behavior
- **Spam**: Unsolicited commercial content or repetitive messages
- **Privacy Violations**: Sharing private information without consent
- **Security Violations**: Encouraging or facilitating actual cyber attacks
- **Disruption**: Deliberate disruption of discussions or project work

## Cybersecurity Ethics

As a cybersecurity-focused project, we have additional ethical expectations:

### Simulation-Only Policy

- **No Real Attacks**: All activities must be simulation-only
- **Educational Focus**: Content must be for educational and defensive purposes
- **Legal Compliance**: All activities must comply with applicable laws
- **Responsible Disclosure**: Follow responsible disclosure practices
- **Safety First**: Prioritize safety and ethical considerations

### Professional Conduct

- **White Hat Only**: Only ethical hacking and security research
- **No Malicious Code**: Do not create or share actual malicious code
- **Privacy Protection**: Respect privacy and data protection laws
- **Attribution**: Give proper credit for work and ideas
- **Integrity**: Maintain honesty and transparency in all work

## Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

1. **Immediate Response**: Contact a project maintainer privately
2. **Documentation**: Document the incident with specific details
3. **Confidentiality**: Reports will be kept confidential
4. **Follow-up**: We will respond within 48 hours

### Contact Information

- **Project Maintainers**: 
  - Create an issue marked "confidential"
  - Email: [maintainer-email@example.com]
- **GitHub**: Use GitHub's private reporting features
- **Community Manager**: [community-manager@example.com]

### Report Content

When reporting, please include:
- **What happened**: Description of the incident
- **When**: Date and time of the incident
- **Who involved**: Individuals involved (if known)
- **Evidence**: Screenshots, logs, or other evidence
- **Impact**: How it affected you or others

## Enforcement Guidelines

### Investigation Process

1. **Initial Review**: Review the report within 48 hours
2. **Investigation**: Conduct a thorough, impartial investigation
3. **Decision**: Make a decision based on evidence and policies
4. **Action**: Take appropriate action based on the decision
5. **Follow-up**: Monitor the situation and follow up as needed

### Possible Actions

Depending on the severity and nature of the violation:

- **Warning**: Private warning for minor infractions
- **Temporary Suspension**: Temporary removal from community
- **Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from community
- **Legal Action**: Involvement of law enforcement if necessary
- **Public Statement**: Public statement about the incident

### Appeals Process

If you believe action taken against you is unfair:

1. **Appeal Request**: Submit a written appeal to project maintainers
2. **Review**: Different maintainers will review the appeal
3. **Decision**: Final decision will be made within 7 days
4. **Communication**: You will be informed of the decision

## Community Guidelines

### Communication Standards

- **Professional Language**: Use professional and respectful language
- **Constructive Feedback**: Provide helpful, constructive feedback
- **Inclusive Language**: Use inclusive and welcoming language
- **Cultural Sensitivity**: Be aware of and respect cultural differences
- **Accessibility**: Make content accessible to all participants

### Technical Discussions

- **Evidence-Based**: Base technical discussions on evidence
- **Open-Minded**: Be open to different perspectives and approaches
- **Educational**: Focus on learning and teaching
- **Safety-Focused**: Prioritize safety and ethical considerations
- **Legal Compliance**: Ensure all discussions comply with applicable laws

### Collaboration Guidelines

- **Credit**: Give proper credit for contributions
- **Transparency**: Be transparent about methods and findings
- **Reproducibility**: Strive for reproducible research and results
- **Documentation**: Document methods and findings thoroughly
- **Peer Review**: Participate constructively in peer review

## Consequences

### Short-term Consequences

- **Warning**: Formal warning for first-time minor violations
- **Temporary Restrictions**: Temporary restrictions on community access
- **Required Training**: Required completion of ethics or conduct training

### Long-term Consequences

- **Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from community for serious violations
- **Legal Action**: Involvement of law enforcement for illegal activities
- **Public Disclosure**: Public disclosure of serious violations
- **Industry Notification**: Notification to industry organizations if appropriate

## Positive Contributions

We encourage and recognize positive contributions:

- **Mentorship**: Helping new community members
- **Education**: Sharing knowledge and expertise
- **Innovation**: Contributing innovative ideas and solutions
- **Community Building**: Helping build a positive community culture
- **Ethical Leadership**: Demonstrating ethical leadership

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to:

- **All Community Spaces**: GitHub, Discord, Slack, email, meetings, etc.
- **All Participants**: Contributors, maintainers, users, and community members
- **All Activities**: Code contributions, discussions, events, and interactions
- **Both Online and Offline**: In-person events and online interactions
